## 1. Selection — The Rule of 5

Five chapters, no more. Diversity beats volume at this stage.

| Slot | Profile | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| 2 | **Friends** — chairs Omm has worked with directly | High trust, will tell you the truth fast. Forgive bugs. |
| 2 | **Introduced strangers** — chairs Omm meets via a mutual Yi connection | Tests if the product sells without Omm's personal credit. |
| 1 | **Wildcard** — a chapter that approaches us, or one that's actively struggling | Real demand signal. Pain-driven adoption is the strongest. |

Five is the right number because: (a) one person can do 5 monthly feedback calls, (b) 5 chapters generate enough usage variance to find the actual breakage points, (c) too few means biased data, too many means shallow learning.

## 2. Target Chapter Profile (Non-Negotiable)

A beta chapter MUST:
- Be **currently running YIP-2026 or YiFuture-2026** (this is the wedge — Free tier hooks them here).
- Have a chair willing to commit to the monthly feedback call personally (not delegate to a secretary).
- Have at least 30 active members (below this, the data is too sparse to test workflows).
- NOT be in the same state as another beta chapter (regional diversity matters for Indian compliance and connectivity testing).

A beta chapter SHOULD:
- Have an active events calendar (3+ events in next 6 months).
- Track finance somewhere already (Excel, Tally, anything) — they will feel the migration pain less.

## 5. Success Threshold

**Month 6 target: 3 of 5 betas converting to paid at ₹20k+ per year.**

This is the gate. Anything below this and the product, the pricing, or the positioning is wrong. We do not proceed to Phase C (national pitch, Pvt Ltd incorporation, paid acquisition) until this is hit.

## 6. Failure Escape Hatch

If **0 of 5 convert** by month 6, freeze new outreach and run a 2-week diagnostic:

1. **Pivot pricing first.** Drop founding chapter rate to ₹5k Starter / ₹15k Pro. See if it's a price wall, not a value wall.
2. **Pivot positioning next.** If price doesn't move them, the product is solving the wrong problem. Re-interview all 5 — what would they pay for? Rebuild the wedge.
3. **Pivot product last.** If positioning is right but they still won't pay, the product has gaps. Cut scope to the one module they actually use daily and rebuild around that.

If **1-2 of 5 convert**, do not pivot. Run cohort 2 (next 5 chapters) with the same pricing and see if the conversion rate holds. One cohort is not a signal.
