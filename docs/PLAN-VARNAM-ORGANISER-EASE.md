# Making the Organiser's Job the Easiest — Evidence-Based Plan

**Date:** 2026-07-14 · **Festival:** ~8 weeks out (Sept, culminating Erode Day Sept 16)
**Question:** Can the app make the Varnam Vizha organiser/coordinator job the easiest?
**Answer:** Yes for ~80% of it — the paperwork, chasing, tracking and reporting. The remaining 20% (persuading sponsors, the Collector relationship, strategy calls) is human by nature. This plan targets the 80%.

## 2. The one insight that changes the design

The 2026 chairs were asked for "a website + Google Sheet" in Feb and it stalled — while 2,669 messages flowed through WhatsApp. **Organisers will not come to the app; the app must come to WhatsApp.** Dashboards are the system of record; WhatsApp is where the work happens. Every phase below ends with "…and it pings WhatsApp."

## 3. What's already built vs the gap

**Built (P0–P5 + PR #788):** public site, registration + custom forms + waitlist + check-in, sponsor pipeline, budget + spend, team management, Playbook, events CRUD. → This is the **system of record**.
**⚠ PR #788 is NOT merged** — the self-serve layer isn't live. Everything starts with merging it.
**Gap:** the **system of work** — tasks/follow-ups, letters, reminders, run-sheets, one-tap reports, WhatsApp delivery.

## 5. "Easiest" as testable promises (how we'll know it worked)

1. Chair opens ONE screen and knows in under a minute: what's late, who owes what, money in vs out.
2. A new coordinator sets up a complete event — with checklist, permission letters and sign-up form — in **under 10 minutes** from a Playbook template.
3. Zero permission letters written by hand. Zero MoMs typed at 1 AM (decisions become tasks live in the meeting).
4. Post-event: P&L + Health Card text in one tap, attendance auto-counted from check-ins.
5. Organisers get everything that matters **in WhatsApp** without being forced into the app.

## 6. What the app cannot make easy (be honest with the committee)

- Persuading Milky Mist to sign — the deck can be generated; the pitch is human.
- The Collector relationship / district-calendar MOU (leverage point #2) — institutional, not software.
- The "do less, better" call — 7 days vs 12, 8–10 anchor events vs 30 (leverage point #3) — a chair's strategic decision the app can *inform* (per-event data finally exists) but not make.
- Replacing the departed designers — the app smooths approvals, not creativity.
- The identity question (chapter activity vs city institution) — decides what "success" even means.
